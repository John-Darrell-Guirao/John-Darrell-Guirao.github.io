Top 6 NFT Arts

You should know

In crypto games, the players own the items acquired in the game and can sell it for cryptocurrency, which can then be exchanged in actual money via exchanges. see more...

1. Beeple’s “Everydays: The First 5000 Days”

This piece is significant for visual artists all over the world because it is the first wholly NFT digital artwork to be auctioned at a major auction house (for a staggering $69 million).

2. William Shatner’s personal memorabilia

William Shatner is a well-known actor with a career spanning over 60 years. He issued a collection of his own artifacts in the form of NFTs in 2020, which includes a wide range of images taken throughout his long career. In 9 minutes, he sold 125,000 units.

3. Nyan Cat GIF

Nyan Cat is a unique piece of online history from the decade of 2010. The video's creator decided to transform it into an NFT and auction it off for its tenth anniversary. The movie was sold for a stunning 300 ETH, which is currently equivalent to around $580,000

4. Jack Dorsey's first-ever tweet

Jack Dorsey, Twitter’s CEO, sold the very first tweet on the platform (made on March 21, 2006) for nearly $3 million as an NFT.According to Dorsey, the money raised from the NFT sale would be converted to bitcoin and then donated to GiveDirectly, a nonprofit that provides cash to individuals living in poverty.

5. Andrés Reisinger’s Virtual Design Objects

Andrés Reisinger, an Argentinian designer, has found a unique market for his work: he offers non-traditional furniture. His most valuable piece for little under $70,000. While this isn't real furniture, it can be used in open environments like Decentraland and Minecraft.

6. RTFKT’s digital sneakers

RTFKT is making a significant splash in the fashion world. People who buy his NFT sneakers are ready to pay upwards of $10,000 for a pair despite the fact that they will never be able to wear them. In March of 2021, RTFKT launched a pair of sneakers he collaborated on with another artist named Fewocious that sold for $3,1 million in a matter of minutes.
