Association with pump and dump schemes:

Pump and dump schemes has been in existence for a long time. Although pump and dump schemes are prohibited on regulated crypto exchanges, it still has its influence on certain cryptos. When a group of people purchase a large amount of coins which increases the price of the asset substantially, that is Pumping. When the coin reaches a higher value than what they were bought for, they release the assets to other buyers and take back a huge return. This type of manipulation in DOGE is quite common.

Poor technical support:

Despite its growing community of users and supporters, Dogecoin has relatively poor technical support. Unlike other cryptocurrencies, the development team of DOGE is not as big as them. Since 2015, there have been no technological updates and changes from DOGE. It is also crucial to highlight the fact that it does not offer any unique selling proposition in terms of technology.

Its fun nature might contradict real innovation:

Since DOGE started and was developed as a joke at existing coins like Bitcoin and Litecoin, This nature obstructs DOGE from integration and adoption as a digital asset.

It is not the best transactional currency:

Dogecoin, unlike other currencies, can only be used as a digital currency. Other currencies such as Tronic and Ether can function as a traditional currency. Cryptos like Bitcoin, Litecoin, Dash, and Bitcoin Cash have more transactional significance in the market than DOGE.
